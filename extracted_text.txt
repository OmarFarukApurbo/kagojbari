PROBLEM LANDSCAPE
Fragmented and often unreliable access to learning
materials, uniforms, and exam resources.
Lack of standardized student identity and data
systems, making it hard to track attendance,
performance, and support.
Bangladesh’s
education system
faces
interconnected
challenges.
Disconnected NGOs, CSR programs, and implementation partners, leading to

children.

Building the Backbone of the Nation
The Backbone School is a flagship education initiative of the Kagojbari ecosystem,
created to expand access to high-quality international education beyond major
cities and into the communities where it is needed most.
While English-medium and globally recognized education opportunities are largely
concentrated in cities like Dhaka, millions of talented students in rural and semi-
urban regions of Bangladesh still lack access to such opportunities.
The Backbone School aims to bridge this gap by establishing a nationwide network
of modern English-medium schools across the country’s upazilas.
The academic program will follow the internationally recognized curriculum of
Pearson Edexcel, ensuring students receive globally competitive education while
remaining connected to their local communities.
The initiative is currently undergoing the regulatory process with the Ministry of
Education Bangladesh. The project will begin with a flagship campus and five pilot
branches to establish a strong academic and operational model. Future expansion
will take place through partnerships with local entrepreneurs, while Kagojbari
provides centralized curriculum systems, teacher training, and quality assurance.
More than a school network, The Backbone School represents a long-term vision:
empowering students across Bangladesh with international-standard education
and preparing the next generation to become the backbone of the nation.

In every nation’s story, there comes a time when vision turns into action. For Bangladesh, 2025
is such a time—when education becomes not just a right, but a national commitment. We
planned it for 2026 currently for political situation in Bangladesh.
This year, Kagojbari has taken a bold step to make history, not only for Bangladesh but for the
world.
We are launching the Guinness World Record Education Material Distribution Campaign, the
first-ever education-focused world record from Bangladesh. Its mission is simple yet powerful:
to deliver essential learning materials to students in marginal areas, ensuring that no child is left
behind in their pursuit of knowledge.
But this is not just about breaking a record.
It is about showing the world that Bangladesh is an education-friendly nation, where collective
effort turns into global recognition. It is about bridging inequality, giving every child an equal
chance to learn, dream, and grow. It is about turning pride into progress, proving that our
future rests in the hands of empowered learners.
This campaign carries a message that goes far beyond numbers—it is a movement, a milestone,
and a mission for change.

A nation does not collapse when its roads break.
A nation does not collapse when its economy slows.
A nation collapses when its education fails.
Education is not one department among many. It is the
system that feeds every other system. Healthcare
depends on educated doctors. Justice depends on
educated judges. Economy depends on educated
workers. Governance depends on educated leaders.
When education is weak, everything built on it is
fragile.
In Bangladesh, we often treat
education as a project. A policy to
launch. A curriculum to revise. A
budget line to adjust. But education
is not a project—it is the foundation.
And foundations cannot be managed
casually.
We build institutions, but we forget
integration. We produce degrees,
but neglect skills. We increase
enrollment, but ignore learning.
We celebrate numbers, but avoid
outcomes.
The result is a silent crisis: students
who pass but cannot perform,
graduates who hold certificates but
lack confidence, and a system that
moves forward in appearance but
backward in impact.
True reform is not loud. It is
disciplined. It happens in
classrooms, not conferences. It
shows in teacher capacity,
not policy speeches.
It is measured in student ability, not
exam statistics.
Education does not need decoration.
It needs direction.
It does not need slogans. It needs
structure.
It does not need sympathy. It needs
seriousness.
If we want a strong Bangladesh, we
must stop treating education as a
social obligation and start treating
it as a national strategy.
Because when education stands
strong, everything stands strong.
And when education breaks, no
development can hold.
Education is not the backbone of
the nation because it is important.
It is the backbone because without
it, nothing else works.
